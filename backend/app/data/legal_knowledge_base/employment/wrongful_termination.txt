TITLE: Wrongful Termination Rights
DOMAIN: EMPLOYMENT
CATEGORY: Employment Rights
TOPICS: wrongful termination, dismissal, employment rights, labor dispute, notice period, employee protection

OVERVIEW:
Employment termination should generally follow the terms of the employment agreement and applicable labor regulations. Employees may challenge termination that appears unfair, discriminatory, retaliatory, or inconsistent with contractual obligations.

EMPLOYEE RIGHTS:

1. Right to Notice
Employees may be entitled to notice or compensation depending on contractual terms and applicable regulations.

2. Right to Explanation
Employees may request reasons for termination where appropriate.

3. Right to Challenge Improper Actions
Employees may seek review or dispute resolution where termination appears improper.

COMMON CONCERNS:

- No notice provided
- Discrimination
- Retaliation
- Breach of contract
- Non-payment of final dues

WHAT EMPLOYEES SHOULD DO:

- Obtain written documentation.
- Preserve emails and communications.
- Maintain performance records.
- Review employment agreements.

IMPORTANT DOCUMENTS:

- Employment contract
- Termination letter
- Salary records
- Performance evaluations
