TITLE: Notice Period Rights
DOMAIN: EMPLOYMENT
CATEGORY: Employment Rights
TOPICS: notice period, employment rights, resignation, termination, salary settlement, employee rights

OVERVIEW:
Notice periods are intended to provide both employers and employees with sufficient time to prepare for the end of an employment relationship. Notice requirements are often specified in employment contracts and company policies.

EMPLOYEE RIGHTS:

1. Right to Review Employment Terms
Employees should review their employment agreement to understand notice period requirements.

2. Right to Salary During Notice
Employees may be entitled to salary and benefits during the notice period, subject to company policies and contractual obligations.

3. Right to Receive Final Settlement
Employers should generally process final dues, leave balances, and other eligible payments according to company policies and applicable regulations.

COMMON SITUATIONS:

- Employee resignation
- Employer termination
- Immediate resignation
- Notice period buyout
- Mutual separation

WHAT EMPLOYEES SHOULD DO:

- Review employment contracts.
- Submit resignation in writing.
- Keep copies of communications.
- Request final settlement details.

IMPORTANT DOCUMENTS:

- Employment agreement
- Resignation letter
- Final settlement statement
- Salary records
