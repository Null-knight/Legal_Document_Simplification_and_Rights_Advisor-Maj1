TITLE: Security Deposit Refund Rights
DOMAIN: TENANT
CATEGORY: Tenant Rights
TOPICS: security deposit, rental deposit, deposit refund, tenant rights, landlord deduction, rental dispute

OVERVIEW:
A security deposit is an amount paid by a tenant to the landlord at the beginning of a tenancy. The deposit is generally intended to cover unpaid rent, damages beyond normal wear and tear, or other obligations specified in the rental agreement.

COMMON TENANT RIGHTS:

1. Right to Deposit Return
Tenants are generally entitled to receive their security deposit back after vacating the property if all contractual obligations have been met.

2. Right to Explanation
If any amount is deducted from the deposit, the tenant may request an explanation and supporting evidence for the deductions.

3. Protection Against Arbitrary Deductions
Landlords should not make unreasonable or unsupported deductions from the deposit.

VALID REASONS FOR DEDUCTION:

- Unpaid rent
- Property damage beyond normal wear and tear
- Unpaid utility bills if specified in the agreement
- Cleaning costs if agreed upon

INVALID OR QUESTIONABLE DEDUCTIONS:

- Normal aging of property
- Minor wear from ordinary use
- Charges not mentioned in the agreement

TENANT BEST PRACTICES:

- Photograph the property before moving in and after moving out.
- Keep copies of rent receipts.
- Obtain written confirmation of property condition.
- Request a final inspection if possible.

IMPORTANT DOCUMENTS:

- Rental agreement
- Deposit payment receipt
- Property photographs
- Written communications
