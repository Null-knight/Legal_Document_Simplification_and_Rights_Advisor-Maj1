TITLE: Landlord Maintenance Obligations
DOMAIN: TENANT
CATEGORY: Tenant Rights
TOPICS: maintenance, repairs, landlord obligations, tenant rights, property maintenance, rental property

OVERVIEW:
Landlords are generally responsible for maintaining a rental property in a condition that is reasonably safe and habitable. Specific responsibilities may vary depending on local laws and the rental agreement.

COMMON LANDLORD RESPONSIBILITIES:

1. Structural Maintenance
Maintaining walls, roofs, doors, windows, and other structural elements.

2. Electrical Safety
Ensuring electrical systems remain reasonably safe and functional.

3. Water Supply
Providing access to basic water facilities where required.

4. Essential Repairs
Addressing major repairs that affect habitability.

5. Common Areas
Maintaining shared spaces in multi-unit properties.

TENANT RESPONSIBILITIES:

- Reporting problems promptly
- Avoiding intentional damage
- Performing basic cleanliness and upkeep

COMMON DISPUTES:

- Delayed repairs
- Water leakage issues
- Electrical faults
- Plumbing problems
- Pest control responsibilities

WHAT TENANTS SHOULD DO:

- Report issues in writing.
- Keep photographs of damage.
- Maintain communication records.
- Retain copies of repair requests.

IMPORTANT DOCUMENTS:

- Rental agreement
- Repair requests
- Photographs
- Inspection reports
