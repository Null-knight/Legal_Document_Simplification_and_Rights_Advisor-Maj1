TITLE: Rent Increase Rules
DOMAIN: TENANT
CATEGORY: Tenant Rights
TOPICS: rent increase, rent revision, tenant rights, rental agreement, housing dispute, landlord notice

OVERVIEW:
Rent increases are generally governed by the rental agreement and applicable housing laws. Landlords should typically provide notice before increasing rent.

TENANT RIGHTS:

1. Right to Notice
Tenants are generally entitled to receive advance notice before a rent increase takes effect.

2. Right to Review Agreement
The rental agreement should be reviewed to determine whether rent revision terms are specified.

3. Protection Against Arbitrary Changes
Sudden or retroactive rent increases may be disputed depending on the circumstances and applicable laws.

FACTORS AFFECTING RENT INCREASES:

- Terms of the rental agreement
- Duration of tenancy
- Local regulations
- Property improvements
- Market conditions

WHAT TENANTS SHOULD DO:

- Review lease terms carefully.
- Request written notice.
- Keep copies of communications.
- Clarify effective dates of changes.

IMPORTANT DOCUMENTS:

- Rental agreement
- Rent increase notice
- Previous payment records
