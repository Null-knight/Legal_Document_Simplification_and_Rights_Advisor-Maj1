TITLE: Online Shopping Disputes
DOMAIN: CONSUMER
CATEGORY: Consumer Rights
TOPICS: online shopping, e-commerce dispute, refund, replacement, consumer complaint, delivery issue

OVERVIEW:
Online shopping disputes may arise when consumers receive incorrect products, damaged goods, delayed deliveries, counterfeit items, or when sellers fail to honor refund and replacement commitments.

COMMON CONSUMER RIGHTS:

1. Right to Receive Ordered Product
Consumers are generally entitled to receive the product that was advertised and purchased.

2. Right to Accurate Information
Product descriptions, specifications, and pricing should not be misleading.

3. Right to Refund or Replacement
Consumers may be entitled to a refund or replacement when the delivered product is defective, damaged, or materially different from what was advertised.

4. Right to Raise Complaints
Consumers can submit complaints through the seller, e-commerce platform, or consumer dispute mechanisms.

COMMON DISPUTES:

- Wrong product delivered
- Damaged product received
- Fake or counterfeit products
- Delivery delays
- Refund not processed
- Seller refusing replacement

WHAT CONSUMERS SHOULD DO:

- Save invoices and order confirmations.
- Take photographs of delivered items.
- Record unboxing when possible.
- Contact customer support immediately.
- Keep screenshots of conversations.

IMPORTANT DOCUMENTS:

- Invoice
- Order confirmation
- Delivery records
- Product photographs
- Customer support communications
