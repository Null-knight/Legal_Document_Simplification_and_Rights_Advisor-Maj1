Overview:

A contract remedy is a legal solution that may be available when one party fails to perform their contractual obligations. The purpose of a remedy is to place the affected party, as far as reasonably possible, in the position they would have been in if the contract had been properly performed.

The available remedy depends on the contract, the nature of the breach, applicable law, and the facts of the dispute.

------------------------------------------------------------

2. Specific Performance

In some situations, a court may require a party to perform the obligations promised in the contract instead of simply paying compensation.

3. Contract Termination

Where permitted by the agreement or applicable law, a party may be able to terminate the contract following a serious breach.

Termination generally ends future contractual obligations but may not remove liability for earlier breaches.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can every breach lead to compensation?

A:

Not necessarily. The available remedy depends on the contract terms, applicable law, evidence, and the nature of the breach.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Can I cancel the contract immediately?

A:

Some contracts permit termination after certain breaches, while others require notice or provide opportunities to correct the problem.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Is negotiation always required?

A:

Not always. However, many disputes are resolved more efficiently through communication or alternative dispute resolution before formal legal proceedings.
