Overview:

Not every agreement is legally enforceable. For a contract to become legally valid, certain essential legal elements must generally be present. If one or more of these elements are missing, the agreement may not be enforceable or may become void or voidable depending on applicable law.

Common Questions:

Q: Is a verbal agreement always a valid contract?

A:
Some verbal agreements may be legally valid depending on the circumstances and applicable law. However, written contracts are generally much easier to prove and enforce.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Can I cancel a contract after signing?

A:
That depends on the contract terms, applicable law, and whether there are legal grounds for cancellation or termination.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What if someone signed under pressure?

A:
A contract signed without free consent may be challenged through appropriate legal processes.

Important Reminder:

Not every signed document automatically becomes legally enforceable. Courts generally examine whether the essential legal requirements of a valid contract are satisfied.
