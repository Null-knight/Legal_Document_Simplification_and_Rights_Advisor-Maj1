Overview:

A rental or lease agreement is a legally binding contract between a property owner (landlord) and a tenant. It sets out the terms under which the tenant may occupy and use the property while defining the rights and responsibilities of both parties.

A written agreement helps prevent misunderstandings and provides evidence if disputes arise.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Should every rental agreement be in writing?

A:

A written agreement provides clearer evidence of the rights and obligations of both parties and is generally preferable to relying solely on verbal arrangements.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Should I keep rent receipts?

A:

Yes. Rent receipts, bank transaction records, and payment confirmations should be preserved throughout the tenancy.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What should I inspect before moving in?

A:

Inspect the property carefully, document existing damage, test utilities and appliances where applicable, and preserve photographs before taking possession.
