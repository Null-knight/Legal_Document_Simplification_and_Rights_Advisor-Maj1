Overview:

An employment contract is a legally binding agreement between an employer and an employee. It defines the rights, responsibilities, duties, benefits, and conditions of employment for both parties.

Employment contracts help reduce misunderstandings by clearly describing the expectations of both the employer and the employee.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is an employment contract always required?

A:

Employment practices vary depending on the employer and applicable law. Written contracts are generally recommended because they clearly define the rights and obligations of both parties.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Can an employer change my salary without updating the contract?

A:

Changes to important employment terms are generally expected to be documented and communicated appropriately. Employees should review any revised agreement before accepting changes.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Should I keep a copy of my employment contract?

A:

Yes. Employees should always keep a signed copy together with related employment documents.
