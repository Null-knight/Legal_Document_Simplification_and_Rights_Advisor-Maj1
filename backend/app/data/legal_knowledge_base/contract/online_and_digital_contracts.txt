Overview:

An online or digital contract is an agreement that is created, accepted, or executed electronically instead of using traditional paper documents. These contracts are commonly used for websites, mobile applications, online shopping, software licenses, cloud services, banking, and digital subscriptions.

Digital contracts have become an important part of everyday life, allowing individuals and businesses to enter agreements quickly and efficiently.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is clicking "I Agree" considered accepting a contract?

A:

Many online services treat clicking an acceptance button as agreeing to the terms presented. The legal effect depends on the agreement, applicable law, and the circumstances.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Should I read online Terms and Conditions?

A:

Yes. Important information about payments, subscriptions, privacy, limitations, and dispute resolution may be included in the agreement.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Should I keep copies of digital agreements?

A:

Yes. Save confirmation emails, invoices, receipts, screenshots, and important documents for future reference.
