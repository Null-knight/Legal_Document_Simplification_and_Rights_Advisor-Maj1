Overview:

A Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA), also called a Confidentiality Agreement, is a contract in which one or more parties agree not to disclose certain confidential information to unauthorized persons.

NDAs are commonly used in employment, business partnerships, software development, research, consulting, investments, and commercial negotiations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is an NDA?

A:

An NDA is a contract that requires one or more parties to keep specified information confidential and use it only for the agreed purpose.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Does an NDA last forever?

A:

Not necessarily. Many NDAs specify a defined confidentiality period, while some obligations may continue depending on the agreement and applicable law.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Can confidential information ever be disclosed?

A:

Some agreements permit disclosure in limited situations, such as when required by law, court order, or where specific contractual exceptions apply.
