Overview:

A freelance or consulting contract is a legally binding agreement between an independent professional and a client. Unlike employment contracts, these agreements generally involve the completion of specific services or projects rather than an employer-employee relationship.

A written contract helps both parties clearly understand expectations, payment terms, ownership of work, deadlines, and dispute resolution procedures.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Should freelancers always use written contracts?

A:

Written contracts clearly define project expectations, payment terms, deliverables, and responsibilities, making them useful for reducing misunderstandings.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Who owns the completed work?

A:

Ownership depends on the agreement. Some contracts transfer ownership after full payment, while others provide only limited usage rights.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Should I request an advance payment?

A:

Many freelance agreements include advance or milestone payments. The payment structure depends on the agreement between the parties.
