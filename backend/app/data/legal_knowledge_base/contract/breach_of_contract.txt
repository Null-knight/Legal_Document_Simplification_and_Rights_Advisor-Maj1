Title: Breach of Contract

DOMAIN: CONTRACT
TOPICS: contract breach, agreement violation, remedies, damages

Category:
Contract Law

Overview:

A breach of contract occurs when one party fails to perform one or more obligations that were agreed upon in a valid contract without a lawful justification.

A breach may involve failing to perform, performing incompletely, performing late, or refusing to perform the agreed obligations.

Types of Breach

1. Minor Breach

A small violation that does not completely destroy the purpose of the agreement.

Example:

A contractor finishes work two days late but completes the project successfully.

------------------------------------------------------------

2. Material Breach

A serious violation that prevents the other party from receiving the main benefit of the contract.

Example:

A company receives payment but never delivers the product.

------------------------------------------------------------

3. Anticipatory Breach

One party clearly states, before the deadline, that they will not perform their obligations.

Example:

A supplier informs the buyer that the ordered goods will never be delivered.

------------------------------------------------------------

4. Actual Breach

The agreed performance date arrives and the party simply fails to perform.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is every broken promise a breach of contract?

A:

Not necessarily. Whether a breach exists depends on the contract terms, applicable law, and the surrounding facts.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Can a contract be terminated after a serious breach?

A:

Some contracts and legal systems allow termination following a material breach, but this depends on the agreement and applicable law.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What should I do first after discovering a breach?

A:

Review the contract, preserve evidence, communicate in writing where appropriate, and understand the available dispute resolution process before taking further action.

------------------------------------------------------------

Important Reminder

A breach of contract does not automatically determine legal liability. The outcome depends on the contract terms, applicable law, available evidence, and the facts of the dispute.
