Overview:

A consumer contract is an agreement between a consumer and a business for the purchase of goods or services. These contracts may be written, electronic, or accepted online through websites and mobile applications.

Consumer contracts generally define the rights and responsibilities of both the buyer and the seller, including payment terms, delivery conditions, warranties, refunds, and dispute resolution procedures.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Should I keep purchase invoices?

A:

Yes. Invoices and payment receipts provide important evidence for warranty claims, refunds, replacements, and future disputes.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What should I do if the delivered product differs from the description?

A:

Preserve photographs, invoices, delivery records, and communications with the seller before requesting an appropriate resolution through the available channels.

------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Are online purchases also consumer contracts?

A:

Yes. Online purchases generally involve consumer contracts, and the applicable rights and obligations depend on the agreement and relevant law.
