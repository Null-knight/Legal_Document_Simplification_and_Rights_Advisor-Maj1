TITLE: Cyber Bullying Reporting Guide
DOMAIN: CYBER
CATEGORY: Cyber Rights
TOPICS: cyber bullying, online harassment, internet abuse, social media harassment, digital safety, cybercrime

OVERVIEW:
Cyber bullying refers to harassment, intimidation, threats, humiliation, or repeated harmful behavior conducted through digital platforms such as social media, messaging applications, gaming platforms, forums, and email.

COMMON FORMS OF CYBER BULLYING:

- Abusive messages
- Threatening messages
- Fake profiles
- Public humiliation
- Rumor spreading
- Sharing private information
- Harassment through repeated communication

WARNING SIGNS:

- Repeated unwanted messages
- Public insults
- Online stalking
- Defamation
- Fake social media accounts

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:

1. Preserve Evidence
Take screenshots and save messages.

2. Block Offenders
Use platform blocking features.

3. Report Content
Use platform reporting tools.

4. Seek Support
Inform trusted family members, friends, teachers, or supervisors.

5. Report Serious Cases
Use official cybercrime reporting channels when necessary.

IMPORTANT EVIDENCE:

- Screenshots
- User profiles
- URLs
- Chat logs
- Email records
