TITLE: Online Blackmail Response Guide
DOMAIN: CYBER
CATEGORY: Cyber Rights
TOPICS: online blackmail, extortion, digital threats, cybercrime, online harassment, internet safety

OVERVIEW:
Online blackmail occurs when someone threatens to reveal private information, photographs, videos, messages, or other sensitive content unless money, favors, or actions are provided.

COMMON METHODS:

- Social media blackmail
- Relationship scams
- Extortion
- Fake investment threats
- Compromised account threats

WHAT TO DO IMMEDIATELY:

1. Do Not Pay
Paying often encourages further demands.

2. Preserve Evidence
Save screenshots and communications.

3. Avoid Further Negotiation
Limit communication with the offender.

4. Secure Accounts
Change passwords and enable additional security.

5. Report Through Appropriate Channels
Use official reporting mechanisms if necessary.

EVIDENCE TO SAVE:

- Screenshots
- Email records
- Usernames
- URLs
- Payment requests
