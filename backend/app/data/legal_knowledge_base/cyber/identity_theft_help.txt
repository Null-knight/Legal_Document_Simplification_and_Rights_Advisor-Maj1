TITLE: Identity Theft Assistance Guide
DOMAIN: CYBER
CATEGORY: Cyber Rights
TOPICS: identity theft, personal information, cybercrime, fraud, account misuse, digital identity

OVERVIEW:
Identity theft occurs when someone uses another person's personal information without authorization for financial, criminal, or fraudulent purposes.

COMMON TYPES:

- Fake bank accounts
- Fraudulent loans
- Unauthorized SIM cards
- Social media impersonation
- Credit card fraud

WARNING SIGNS:

- Unknown transactions
- Unexpected loan notices
- Unauthorized account creation
- Unusual messages from contacts

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:

1. Contact Relevant Institutions
Notify banks, service providers, or affected organizations.

2. Preserve Evidence
Collect screenshots, emails, and transaction records.

3. Secure Accounts
Change passwords and enable additional security measures.

4. Report Incidents
Use official reporting channels where appropriate.

IMPORTANT DOCUMENTS:

- Identity proof
- Transaction records
- Communication logs
- Fraud evidence
